Enforcement Signals and Case Highlights

Europe

  • Spain: Vueling Airlines received a fine of ninety thousand euro and a temporary ban on certain public funds for an inaccessible site.
  • Norway: SAS faced daily fines until accessibility issues were fixed. Separately, the US Department of Transportation fined SAS for web rule violations under the Air Carrier Access Act.
  • EU wide: the European Accessibility Act applies from 28 June 2025 to covered products and services.

United States

  • Target settled a class action for six million dollars and committed to WCAG conformance.
  • Netflix entered a consent decree to caption all streaming content and paid attorney fees and monitoring costs.
  • H&R Block entered a DOJ consent decree requiring WCAG 2.0 AA for web and mobile with civil penalties.
  • Domino's lost at the Ninth Circuit, the Supreme Court declined review, and the case later settled. The ruling confirmed ADA coverage for sites and apps tied to physical stores.

What We Cover in the US

  • ADA Title III expectations for businesses and recent case law for websites and apps
  • ADA Title II for state and local governments with new DOJ rule adopting WCAG 2.1 AA and compliance dates based on entity size
  • Larger jurisdictions: April 24, 2026
  • Smaller jurisdictions and special districts: April 26, 2027
  • Section 508 for federal agencies and procurement, aligned to WCAG 2.0 AA

What We Cover in the EU

  • Web Accessibility Directive 2016/2102
  • European Accessibility Act 2019/882
  • Spain specific coverage (1112/2018 which transposes the Web Accessibility Directive and transposed EAA)

How a7d Maps to EU Obligations
